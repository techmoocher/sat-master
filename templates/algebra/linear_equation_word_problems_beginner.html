Linear Equation Word Problems

Learn how to translate real-world scenarios into linear equations

Translating Words to Equations

One of the most common types of problems on the SAT Math section involves translating word problems into linear equations. These problems test your ability to identify variables, establish relationships, and solve for unknowns.

Key Problem-Solving Steps
  1. Identify what you're solving for and assign a variable (e.g., x)
  2. Identify any other unknowns in terms of your variable
  3. Translate the word problem into an equation
  4. Solve the equation
  5. Check if your answer makes sense in the context of the problem

Common Types of Word Problems

Age Problems

Example: John is 5 years older than Maria. The sum of their ages is 37. How old is each person?

Step 1: Assign a variable.

Let x = Maria's age

Step 2: Express John's age in terms of x.

John's age = x + 5

Step 3: Write an equation based on the given information.

Maria's age + John's age = 37

x + (x + 5) = 37

2x + 5 = 37

Step 4: Solve for x.

2x = 32

x = 16

Step 5: Find both ages.

Maria's age = 16

John's age = 16 + 5 = 21

Step 6: Verify.

16 + 21 = 37 ✓

Money Problems

Example: Sarah has $5 bills and $10 bills in her wallet. She has a total of 12 bills worth $95. How many of each type of bill does she have?

Step 1: Assign a variable.

Let x = number of $5 bills

Step 2: Express the number of $10 bills in terms of x.

Number of $10 bills = 12 - x

Step 3: Write an equation based on the total value.

5x + 10(12 - x) = 95

5x + 120 - 10x = 95

-5x + 120 = 95

Step 4: Solve for x.

-5x = -25

x = 5

Step 5: Find the number of each type of bill.

Number of $5 bills = 5

Number of $10 bills = 12 - 5 = 7

Step 6: Verify.

5 + 7 = 12 bills ✓

$5 × 5 + $10 × 7 = $25 + $70 = $95 ✓

Mixture Problems

Example: A chemist needs to create 100 mL of a 25% acid solution. If she has a 10% acid solution and a 50% acid solution, how much of each should she mix?

Step 1: Assign a variable.

Let x = amount of 10% solution (in mL)

Step 2: Express the amount of 50% solution in terms of x.

Amount of 50% solution = 100 - x mL

Step 3: Write an equation based on the amount of pure acid.

Amount of acid from 10% solution + Amount of acid from 50% solution = Amount of acid in final solution

0.10x + 0.50(100 - x) = 0.25(100)

0.10x + 50 - 0.50x = 25

-0.40x + 50 = 25

Step 4: Solve for x.

-0.40x = -25

x = 62.5

Step 5: Find the amount of each solution.

Amount of 10% solution = 62.5 mL

Amount of 50% solution = 100 - 62.5 = 37.5 mL

Practice Questions

1. The sum of three consecutive integers is 72. What is the smallest of these integers?

Let x = the smallest integer Then x+1 = the next integer, and x+2 = the third integer The sum is 72, so: x + (x+1) + (x+2) = 72 3x + 3 = 72 3x = 69 x = 23 The smallest integer is 23.

2. Tickets for a school play cost $8 for adults and $5 for students. The total revenue from ticket sales was $2,340 and 350 tickets were sold. How many student tickets were sold?

Let x = number of student tickets Then 350 - x = number of adult tickets Total revenue equation: 5x + 8(350 - x) = 2,340 5x + 2,800 - 8x = 2,340 -3x + 2,800 = 2,340 -3x = -460 x = 153.33... Since we can't have a fractional number of tickets, let's check if there's a mistake. If x = 153 students: 5(153) + 8(350-153) = 765 + 8(197) = 765 + 1,576 = 2,341 If x = 154 students: 5(154) + 8(350-154) = 770 + 8(196) = 770 + 1,568 = 2,338 The closest answer is 154 student tickets.

SAT Tips for Word Problems

  • Read the problem carefully and identify the unknown quantities.
  • Draw diagrams or tables to organize information when needed.
  • Use clear variable assignments and be consistent with units.
  • Always verify your answer in the context of the original problem.
  • Check if the answer is reasonable (e.g., no negative ages, fractional people, etc.).
