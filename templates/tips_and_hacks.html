SAT Tips & Hacks

Proven strategies and shortcuts to boost your SAT score

The Ultimate SAT Toolkit

Welcome to our collection of powerful tips, time-saving strategies, and effective shortcuts that can help you approach the SAT with confidence. These techniques have been compiled from top-performing students, experienced tutors, and official test-taking experts.

Pro Tip: Combine these strategies with consistent practice for maximum effectiveness. Remember, knowing the right approach is only half the battle - applying it under test conditions is equally important.

General Test Strategies

Time Management

  • Skip difficult questions and return to them later
  • Spend no more than 1-2 minutes per question initially
  • Mark questions you're unsure about to revisit if time permits
  • Always leave 5 minutes at the end to review your answers

Process of Elimination

  • Cross out obviously wrong answers immediately
  • For every answer choice eliminated, your guess probability improves
  • Look for extreme language like "always" or "never"
  • When two answers are similar, usually neither is correct

Mental Preparation

  • Get 8 hours of sleep the night before the test
  • Eat a balanced breakfast with protein on test day
  • Practice breathing exercises if you feel anxious
  • Visualize success and remind yourself of your preparation

Math Section Hacks

Calculator Strategies

  • Use the calculator to check your work, not to solve everything
  • Program common formulas into your calculator before test day
  • Use estimation to check if your calculator answer is reasonable
  • For questions with complex calculations, try using answer choices

Quick Estimation

  • Round numbers to make mental math easier
  • Use estimation to eliminate obviously wrong answers
  • When working with percentages, convert to fractions when possible
  • Remember that √2 ≈ 1.4 and π ≈ 3.14 for quick calculations

Problem-Solving Shortcuts

  • For systems of equations, try substituting answer choices
  • Draw diagrams for geometry and word problems
  • Double-check your answers by plugging them back into the original problem
  • For multiple choice, try working backwards from the answers

Common Traps to Avoid

  • Don't forget to convert units if necessary
  • Watch out for negative numbers in quadratics and absolute values
  • Don't mistake the median for the mean or average
  • Remember that correlation doesn't imply causation in data questions

Reading & Writing Hacks

Reading Passage Strategy

  • Skim the questions before reading the passage
  • Read the introduction and conclusion paragraphs carefully
  • Pay special attention to the first and last sentences of each paragraph
  • Actively annotate or underline key ideas as you read

Evidence-Based Questions

  • For paired questions, solve the second question first to find the evidence
  • Double-check that your selected evidence actually supports your answer
  • Focus on what the passage directly states, not what you infer
  • Watch for subtle shifts in tone or perspective

Writing Section Tips

  • Read the entire sentence before making a correction
  • Check for subject-verb agreement errors
  • Look for consistency in pronoun usage and tense
  • Remember that the shortest correct answer is often best

Passage Organization

  • Pay attention to transition words that signal relationships between ideas
  • For "best location" questions, check how the sentence connects to ideas before and after
  • For conclusion questions, ensure the answer ties back to the main point
  • When asked about adding or deleting information, check if it's relevant to the main idea

Test Day Reminders

What to Bring:
  • Admission ticket & photo ID
  • Several #2 pencils & good eraser
  • Approved calculator with fresh batteries
  • Watch (without alarms or smart features)
  • Snacks and water for breaks
What to Avoid:
  • Staying up late the night before
  • Skipping breakfast on test day
  • Arriving at the test center late
  • Studying new material right before the test
  • Overthinking or second-guessing yourself
